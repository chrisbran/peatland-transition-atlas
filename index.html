Method and uncertainty

How to read this prototype

This Atlas is a literature-driven prototype. It is designed to make evidence, assumptions and transition hypotheses visible, not to replace local hydrological-economic modelling.

Qualitative scores

Scores are literature-informed expert codings, not statistical estimates. The scale is deliberately simple: 1 = low, 2 = medium, 3 = high.

Evidence-map points

Map points are approximate visual anchors for study regions, national model studies, review clusters and policy nodes. They are not exact field-site coordinates.

South Germany fit

South Germany fit is a transfer hypothesis based on international literature. It is not a validated regional model result and must be checked against local hydrology, farm structure, machinery access and markets.

Publication boundary

The public repository should contain code, derived metadata and own coding tables. It should not contain copyrighted PDFs, confidential project data, licence-unclear raw rasters or unpublished partner data.
