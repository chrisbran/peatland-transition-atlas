## Editorial changes

### 1. Main messages shortened

The story-card messages were tightened to improve scanability and avoid dense card text.

### 2. Stronger conceptual through-line

The revised storyline now moves more clearly from spatial hotspot framing to hydrological mechanism, then to evidence, pathways, regional transfer and modelling.

### 3. Claims made more cautious

South Germany statements are framed as transfer hypotheses, not validated regional conclusions.

### 4. Better distinction between evidence and decision support

The text now clarifies that the Atlas translates literature into structured evidence and hypotheses, not into definitive prescriptions.

## Revised section messages

1. **The hidden climate hotspot**  
   Drained agricultural peatlands are climate hotspots because peat, drainage and production overlap.

2. **The water table is the lever**  
   The decisive variable is not peatland area alone, but achievable water-table depth.

3. **Evidence from elsewhere**  
   International evidence does not provide one solution; it provides transferable lessons.

4. **The transition space**  
   The design space is a spectrum of wetter land-use pathways, each with different trade-offs.

5. **What fits South Germany?**  
   South Germany needs transition pathways that connect mitigation with recognisable farm systems.

6. **The modelling frontier**  
   The next decision tools must connect hydrology, GHG mitigation, farm economics and viable land use.
